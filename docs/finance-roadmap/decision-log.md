# Finance Roadmap Decision Log

Record durable implementation decisions here so future sessions do not re-argue settled architecture.

| Date | Decision | Why It Was Made | Follow-up |
|---|---|---|---|
| 2026-04-01 | Settlement truth moves from `SO` to `SI` and from `PO` to `VB` once the finance document exists. | Orders are operational/commercial documents; issued/post finance documents are the legal/financial truth. | Preserve this in all new settlement, reminder, and reporting work. |
| 2026-04-01 | Pre-existing order-linked receipts/payments must be re-associated to the finance document when the finance document is created. | Prevent duplicate exposure and keep one active settlement anchor. | Keep validating this in future regression coverage. |
| 2026-04-03 | Sales invoice corrections support partial and cumulative credit notes. | Full-only reversal is too primitive for real AR operations. | Keep invoice state and outstanding logic cumulative. |
| 2026-04-03 | Sales invoice corrections also support debit notes and mixed credit/debit adjustment chains. | AR needs upward legal corrections, not only downward reversals. | Treat `current legal amount` as original minus credits plus debits. |
| 2026-04-03 | The lower-left invoice output card now carries `Motivo de isenção do IVA` instead of the old fiscal summary card. | VAT exemption reason is the more relevant commercial/compliance field for Mozambique output. | Keep this field explicit on issue and output flows. |
| 2026-04-04 | AP uses the same maturity model as AR where justified: vendor bills as anchors, supplier credit notes to reduce liability, supplier debit notes to increase liability. | AP was materially underbuilt compared with AR and needed coherent parity. | Keep AP parity focused on finance coherence, not blind symmetry. |
| 2026-04-04 | Supplier invoice reference and Stockwise internal reference are separate concepts. | Supplier reference originates externally; the internal key is Stockwise audit/system identity. | New UX must keep this distinction explicit and avoid ambiguous prefixes. |
| 2026-04-04 | Legacy `COD-*` internal references remain for audit continuity, but new internal AP references use clear prefixes such as `VB`, `VCN`, and `VDN`. | `COD` is a legacy company-derived prefix, not a finance document-type code. | Avoid introducing unexplained prefixes on new finance screens. |
| 2026-04-04 | Due reminders must be redesigned to follow the active finance anchor once an invoice exists. | Sales-order reminders contradict the settlement-anchor model after invoice issuance. | Track this in Phase 3 until implemented. |
| 2026-04-04 | Customer due reminders now follow the active AR anchor: sales order before issuance, sales invoice after issuance. | Reminder truth must follow the same legal/financial anchor as settlement truth and current legal outstanding. | Keep reminder rows anchored to invoice outstanding and invoice language snapshot when an issued invoice exists. |
| 2026-04-04 | Document language behavior remains a tracked gap. | The app supports `pt`/`en` selection and stores document language, but output helpers still render Portuguese-first. | Resolve source-of-truth and implement bilingual output in a future roadmap item. |
| 2026-04-04 | Finance-document output language is snapshot-first, with app/document language as fallback only when no snapshot exists. | Issued/post output must remain stable, while unsnapshotted documents still need to follow the currently selected language. | Keep HTML, PDF, print, and share output on the same bilingual helper and do not reintroduce Portuguese-only labels. |
| 2026-04-04 | Engineering roadmap tracking stays repo-first for now. | Current Settings and Mozambique Compliance screens are tenant-facing operational/compliance surfaces, not the right place for engineering execution status. | If in-app visibility is ever needed, add a restricted internal/admin route rather than cluttering tenant-facing UI. |
| 2026-04-04 | Phase 1 permissions use the current company role model with an approval checkpoint on base AR/AP documents. | This delivers enforceable control without inventing a second role system or premature escalation complexity. | Keep `OPERATOR+` for draft preparation and approval submission; keep `ADMIN+` for approval, legal issue/post, void, adjustment, settlement-sensitive actions, and due-reminder policy changes. |
| 2026-04-08 | Phase 2 finance audit uses a shared journal model plus UI fallback synthesis for older records. | Existing live documents predated some of the richer event triggers, but finance users still need a coherent history without rewriting historical legal documents. | Keep DB journaling authoritative for new events, but allow detail pages to synthesize missing create/approval/adjustment/settlement steps from stored timestamps and linked rows when older records lack those event rows. |
| 2026-04-08 | Parent invoice and vendor-bill histories must explicitly include related adjustment creation/posting and settlement events. | Header-only lifecycle rows are not enough for finance review; users need to see how the legal amount changed and when cash/bank activity hit the active anchor. | Preserve the parent-adjustment and settlement event triggers and keep the activity journal focused on actor, time, transition, and related-document context. |
| 2026-04-08 | Adjustment documents now require structured reason codes alongside optional narrative detail. | Free text alone is weak for audit review and reporting; finance needs consistent categories without losing operational detail. | Keep reason code mandatory on AR/AP credit/debit note flows and surface both the structured label and free-text explanation in chain and table views. |
| 2026-04-08 | Native sales-invoice drafts now auto-bootstrap the minimum Mozambique fiscal settings and current-year fiscal series when the company has not been configured yet. | The draft header trigger allocates the legal reference immediately, so draft creation was failing on `company_fiscal_settings_missing` before users could even open the fiscal invoice for eligible sales orders. | Keep draft creation tolerant of missing fiscal defaults, but keep issue-time seller tax identity and legal snapshot validation strict. |
| 2026-04-08 | Purchase-order to vendor-bill actions now follow one deterministic state model across list and detail surfaces. | The draft-creation RPC already existed, but the UI still mixed legacy status checks, missing billability explanations, and stale existing-bill handling, which left approved POs looking ambiguous or dead-ended. | Keep the VB action state derived from approved workflow, billable lines, existing bill linkage, and current permission; if a stale create attempt collides with an existing bill, refresh and open that bill instead of stopping on an error. |
| 2026-04-09 | PO billability is separate from receipt status. | The prior action state still treated active `purchase_order_lines` as the billing source, while receipt cleanup could remove fully received lines. That wrongly made physical receipt suppress AP billing. | One Vendor Bill per PO remains the current policy: if no draft/posted bill exists, an approved PO with positive purchased value remains billable even when partially or fully received; if a bill exists, open it instead of creating a duplicate. |
